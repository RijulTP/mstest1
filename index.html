The engine can generate 3D scenes with multiple objects, light sources, and camera perspectives. The engine uses Raytracing to simulate the path of light as it interacts with objects in the scene. It calculates the color of each pixel by tracing the path of light from the camera through the scene and calculating the color of the object that the ray intersects. The engine also implements various optimization techniques, such as BVH (Bounding Volume Hierarchy) and multithreading, to improve the rendering speed. I used Peter Shirley's book as a guide to develop the Raytracing engine, implementing various techniques such as anti-aliasing, motion blur, and depth of field.
